Bird Watching

Details on Bird Watching

The highest point in Bedok Reservoir Park promises magnificent panoramic views of the reservoir. There are many shelters and benches available for resting so you need not worry too much about the climb up making it a perfect activity even for the old.


Here are some unique birds that you may see at the park

Collared Kingfisher

See if you can spot the Collared Kingfisher(Todiramphus chloris). It has a bluish-green head,upperparts and wings, and a white collar adjoining white underparts. This is a conspicuous and noisy bird that feeds on crabs, worms, large insects and lizards. It is a wetland bird and appears mostly in tidal mangroves, sandy beaches, gardens and parks

White-throated Kingfisher

Another kingfisher which you can look out for is the White-throated Kingfisher (Halcyon smyrnensis). It has a brown head and belly; white throat and breast resembling a bib; shimmering blue back and tail; and a red bill and feet. It seems to prefer small crustaceans and insects to fish. It usually takes its prey off the ground, sometimes hovering to locate a target, landing among tall grass first, then killing its prey with its powerful bill.

Whitebreasted Waterhen

Another common bird at this park is the Whitebreasted Waterhen (Amaurornis phoenicurus). This is a dark grey bird with a clean white face, breast and belly. Its loud quarrelsome calls are more often heard at dawn and at dusk. It is a wary and alert bird with a habit of bobbing its reddish-brown undertail coverts continuously when feeding or disturbed.
